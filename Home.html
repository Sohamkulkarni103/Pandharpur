Hair Treatments


Hereditary hair loss, also known as androgenetic alopecia or pattern hair loss, is the most common form of hair loss in both men and women. It’s caused by genetic factors and can be inherited from either the mother’s or father’s side of the family. While hereditary hair loss cannot be completely prevented, there are medical treatments that can help maintain, improve, and optimize hair growth. Here are some options:


 Topical Minoxidil: This FDA-approved medication is available for both men and women. It is applied directly to the scalp and can promote hair growth by increasing blood flow to hair follicles.

 Finasteride: This oral medication is primarily used for men. It works by blocking the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which is associated with hair loss. Finasteride can help slow down hair thinning and promote regrowth.

 Spironolactone: Although primarily used as a diuretic, spironolactone has anti-androgen effects and may be prescribed off-label for women with androgenetic alopecia.

 Hair Transplant Surgery: For more advanced cases, hair transplantation is an effective option. During this procedure, hair follicles are taken from areas of the scalp with healthy hair and transplanted to areas with thinning or balding.

 Lifestyle Changes: Reducing stress, maintaining a balanced diet, and practicing good hair care can also contribute to healthier hair.

IV glutathione (skin whitening)


Intravenous (IV) glutathione for skin whitening is a controversial procedure that involves administering high doses of glutathione intravenously to lighten the skin. Glutathione is a powerful antioxidant naturally produced in the body and is involved in various cellular processes, including tissue building and repair, immune function, and detoxification.

The practice of using IV glutathione for skin whitening gained popularity primarily in Asia, particularly in countries like the Philippines and Thailand, where fair skin is often associated with beauty. It has also gained some traction in other parts of the world.

Proponents of IV glutathione for skin whitening claim that it can lighten the skin by reducing melanin production. Melanin is the pigment responsible for skin coloration, and reducing its production can result in a lighter complexion. Glutathione is believed to achieve this by inhibiting the enzyme tyrosinase, which is involved in the production of melanin.

However, the efficacy and safety of IV glutathione for skin whitening are still subjects of debate. While some individuals report seeing results after undergoing the treatment, others may not experience noticeable changes. Additionally, there are potential risks and side effects associated with the procedure, including:

Ineffectiveness: IV glutathione may not produce the desired skin lightening effects for everyone. Results can vary based on factors such as individual skin type, melanin levels, and overall health.

Side Effects: Common side effects of IV glutathione may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and allergic reactions. In some cases, it can also cause more serious adverse effects such as kidney damage, liver dysfunction, and electrolyte imbalances.

Regulatory Concerns: The use of IV glutathione for skin whitening is not approved by regulatory authorities like the FDA (Food and Drug Administration) in the United States for cosmetic purposes. In some countries, it may be regulated as a prescription medication or banned altogether due to safety concerns.

Long-term Safety: There is limited research on the long-term safety of using IV glutathione for skin whitening. Prolonged use of high doses of glutathione may potentially lead to imbalances in the body's antioxidant system and other adverse health effects.

Unregulated Market: In many places where IV glutathione is offered for skin whitening, the market is largely unregulated. This can lead to issues with the quality and safety of the products used, as well as inadequate monitoring of patients undergoing the treatment.


Due to these concerns, many healthcare professionals caution against the use of IV glutathione for skin whitening, advocating for safer and more evidence-based approaches to skincare and skin lightening. It's essential for individuals considering this treatment to thoroughly research the potential risks and benefits and consult with a qualified healthcare provider before proceeding

Concerns


Acne

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when your hair follicles become plugged with oil and dead skin cells. It causes whiteheads, blackheads or pimples. Acne is most common among teenagers, though it affects people of all ages. Effective acne treatments are available, but acne can be persisten.


Eczema

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a condition that causes dry, itchy and inflamed skin. It's common in young children but can occur at any age. Atopic dermatitis is long lasting (chronic) and tends to flare sometimes. It can be irritating but it's not contagious.

Hives

Hives, or urticaria, can occur for many reasons, such as exposure to an allergen or a physical trigger, like pressure from tight clothing. It may also indicate an infection or another underlying health condition.


Psoriasis

Psoriasis is an autoimmune disease that causes plaques, which are itchy or sore patches of thick, dry, discolored skin. While any part of your body can be affected, psoriasis plaques most often develop on the elbows, knees, scalp, back, palms, and feet. Like other autoinflammatory diseases, psoriasis occurs when your immune system — which normally attacks infectious germs — begins to attack healthy cells instead.

Latex allergy

Latex allergy is a reaction to certain proteins found in natural rubber latex, a product made from the rubber tree. If you have a latex allergy, your body mistakes latex for a harmful substance.

Cellulitis

Cellulitis is a common infection of the skin, almost always caused by bacteria. The infection can come on suddenly and usually spreads from the skin surface (epidermis) and then goes deeper into the dermis and the subcutaneous fat layers.
